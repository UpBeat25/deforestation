Deforestation [ˌdiːfɒrɪˈsteɪʃ(ə)n]:

Deforestation refers to the large-scale removal of forested areas, often resulting in damage to the quality of the land. Forests are cleared for agriculture, urban development, and logging. This process has significant impacts on biodiversity, climate change, and the livelihood of communities.

Deforestation Rates Over the Years

Causes of Deforestation:

• Agriculture: Conversion of forests to agricultural land to feed a growing population.

• Logging: Harvesting timber for wood, paper, and fuel.

• Urbanization: Expanding cities and infrastructure development.

• Mining: Clearing forests for minerals and resources extraction.

Main Causes of Deforestation

Impacts of Deforestation:

• Climate Change: Conversion of forests to agricultural land to feed a growing population.

• Loss of Biodiversity: Harvesting timber for wood, paper, and fuel.

• Soil Erosion: Expanding cities and infrastructure development.

• Displacement of Communities: Clearing forests for minerals and resources extraction.

Impacts on Biodiversity

Solutions to Deforestation:

• Reforestation: Planting trees to replace those that have been cut down.

• Sustainable Practices: Promoting sustainable agricultural and logging practices.

• Protected Areas: Establishing national parks and reserves.

• Legislation: Enforcing laws to prevent illegal logging and land clearance.
